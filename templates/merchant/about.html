Get paid even when your Wi-Fi drops.

Auguspay is a Progressive Web App that lets kirana merchants keep issuing dynamic, reconcilable UPI QR codes even when the shop's internet is down.

What it is

A phone-based point-of-sale app. Every time a customer pays, your phone mints a fresh QR with a signed token, the bank confirms the payment, and your dashboard auto-flips to ✅ PAID.

📡

Works offline

Mint QRs locally with cached HMAC secret. Internet drop ≠ lost sale.

⚡

Live confirmation

Bank webhook arrives → screen flips green + beep. No trusting screenshots.

📒

Honest ledger

Every sale labelled paid / pending / disputed / unmatched at day-end.

How it works

  1. Sign up once — shop name, UPI VPA, phone. Server gives your device a secret key. Install to home screen.
  2. Customer pays — type amount → fresh QR appears → customer scans & pays from any UPI app.
  3. Bank confirms — webhook fires → your dashboard flips to ✅ PAID with a beep, in about 2–5 seconds.
  4. End of day — open the ledger, see every sale matched to a bank UTR. Download CSV for accounting.

Security model

Every QR carries a 22-character signed token:

token = base64url( HMAC_SHA256( merchant_secret,
        "<merchant_id>|<seq>|<amount_paise>|<nonce>" ) )[:22]
  • The token rides along with the payment in the UPI tr field.
  • When the bank webhook arrives, the server verifies the signature with your secret — fake claims are rejected.
  • Webhook calls themselves are HMAC-signed (X-Auguspay-Signature) so a stranger can't forge settlements.
  • The same UTR is idempotent — duplicate webhook fires don't double-count.

Status meanings on the ledger

PAID

Claim + settlement both present, amounts match. Real money in your account.

PENDING

You recorded the sale, the bank hasn't confirmed yet. Usually resolves in seconds.

DISPUTED

Both present, amounts disagree. Investigate manually.

UNMATCHED

Bank reported money you didn't mint a QR for. Direct transfer or possible fraud.

Honest FAQ

Is this really offline payments?

No. Money cannot move without UPI rails being online. What can be offline is the merchant's device. The customer's phone still needs data to pay. That's the realistic kirana scenario: shop WiFi is down, but the customer walking in has 4G.

How is this different from a printed sticker QR?

Static QRs let any customer pay any amount with no link back to which sale it was. Auguspay generates a fresh amount-bound, signed QR for each sale, so you can prove who paid what and detect fraud at end-of-day.

How is this different from GPay / PhonePe Business?

Those apps require merchant-side internet to generate a dynamic-amount QR. Auguspay generates the QR locally on your phone using a cached secret — so internet drop doesn't downgrade you to static QRs.

How does the bank actually confirm a payment?

Through your PSP's webhook (Razorpay / Cashfree / Decentro). They post settlement details to /merchant/api/webhook/upi. The server verifies the signature, stores the UTR, and pushes a live event to your dashboard over Server-Sent Events.

What if the device is completely offline when a customer pays?

Mint the QR, tap "Mark paid (offline fallback)" after the customer shows you their UPI app's success screen. The claim queues in IndexedDB. When you next get internet, claims sync and reconcile against bank webhooks automatically.

What data is stored where?

On your phone: a HMAC secret (in IndexedDB) and any queued offline claims. On the server: merchant profile, minted tokens, claims, settlements. Nothing is shared with third parties.
